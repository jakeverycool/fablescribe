# Fablescribe

**An AI storytelling companion for Dungeon Masters.**

Fablescribe listens to your D&D sessions over Discord and helps you bring your world to life — voicing your NPCs, remembering what happened in past sessions, and keeping your campaign lore organized and searchable.

---

## What it does

### 🎙️ Listens to your session
Fablescribe's Discord bot joins your voice channel and transcribes everything said at the table in real time. You get a live chat log of the whole session, separated by speaker, so nothing gets lost — not the clever plan the rogue whispered, not the tavern keeper's name a player improvised on the spot.

### ✨ Voices your NPCs with AI
When a player walks up to an innkeeper, a guard, or a wizened oracle, you can generate an in-character response on the spot. Pick the character, Fablescribe writes their dialogue in their voice and personality, you edit it if you want, and then it plays through the Discord channel in a real AI-generated voice of your choice. Every NPC can have their own distinct voice.

### 🧠 Remembers your campaign
As you play, you pick out the moments that matter — a key conversation, a promise made, a secret revealed — and save them to your **campaign memory**. Over time, this becomes the living history of your world.

When you need to recall something from sessions ago, you can just ask: *"What did Gareth the innkeeper tell us about the missing shipment?"* or *"Who in the party knows about the cursed altar?"* Fablescribe searches your campaign memory and gives you the answer.

### 📚 Keeps your world organized
Build out characters, places, factions, items, and lore in a flexible glossary. No stat blocks or rigid templates — just your notes, in your words, connected however you want. The more you add, the richer your NPCs become when Fablescribe voices them, because they'll actually *know* the things their character would know.

### 📎 Stores your campaign files
Upload the stuff you already have — player handouts, maps, homebrew PDFs, inspiration images, session prep notes — and keep it all attached to your campaign in one place. Down the road, Fablescribe will be able to read your uploaded documents and let the AI reference them too, so that 40-page homebrew setting doc you wrote can actually inform how your NPCs talk.

### 👥 Lets players follow along
Invite your players to your campaign and they can review the curated story log between sessions — a great way to catch up after missing a game or remind everyone what happened last time.

---

## Who it's for

DMs who want to:
- **Make their NPCs feel more alive** without needing to do 20 different accents
- **Stop forgetting** what that one merchant said three sessions ago
- **Spend less time** flipping through handwritten notes mid-session
- **Build a rich, continuous campaign world** that players can actually explore and remember

You don't need to be technical. You don't need to change how you run your game. Fablescribe sits alongside your session and helps when you want help.

---

## What it's *not*

Fablescribe isn't a virtual tabletop. It doesn't track HP, initiative, inventory, or dice rolls. There are plenty of great tools for that already. Fablescribe is focused on the **story** side of D&D — the voices, the memories, the world.

---

## Where it's going

Fablescribe is early in development. The first version is focused on the core loop: live transcription, NPC voice generation, campaign memory, and the memory chatbot. Future ideas on the table include voice cloning for recurring characters, smarter cross-session linking, player-facing search, and more — but I'd love to hear what *you* would want from a tool like this.
